  • I have been working as a president of my high school club called Multicultural Interaction of China since September of 2020. To establish a good atmosphere and relationship among club members, I have organized three online electric sports events from October 2020 to April 2021. The idea of organizing online events came to my mind when we had routine meetings among club leaders. I found it hard to get close to most of the club members because of the pandemic. Therefore, I came up with the idea to play popular phone games to have members be involved. I set up a meeting among leaders to decide which game to play. Opinions differed, so I suggested voting for the final decision. At the end of the session, we chose Honor of Kings, famous both in China and America. For other ideas, we discussed using them for later events to diversify the content of events. While preparing the event, I divided the entire work into parts, including register, publicity, and execution. To avoid external assistance to make the game unfair, I asked the registering member to set up a registering form including personal information to check at the beginning of the game. I arranged publicity plans with poster design and posted them on the club website to get more attention from club members. The art chair designed a poster containing attractive game information and prize for winners. When the game started, the vice president and I were in charge of controlling the entire situation. Another leader was involved in recording climax moments and results to present on the club website and send to members. At the end of the game, the winner was announced with a special prize. Other participators got the bonus to have discounts on milk tea in which milk tea shop sponsored the club. With my effort to lead my leader team to organize well, this online event was unexpectedly successful so that more club members would like to participate and look forward to later activities.

  • I believe that I am a person with a creative side in computer science and able to solve facing problems with innovative thinking. I have discovered my creation while learning Computer Science both at the college course and self-improvement learning. To utilize skills and programming capacities in the practical section, I started to look for opportunities in my life and campus work. As I became the president of the MIC club in my high school, I found there was a lack of information presentation methods to record significant events and publicize the club. Combining my interest and acquirement in programming and computer science studies, I decided to create a website. At the beginning of the website building, I faced challenges though I have practiced a lot since I started my CS learning, such as the difference between a club website from an e-commerce website I have attempted to make before. I researched various designs of web pages for navigation bars, layout, and submenu settings. After that, I analyzed the usages of my club website, which I knew the best. Later as I have built up the primary website, I found some user interfaces seemed dull without transition and special effects. So I worked to optimize the code to provide multiple submenus and pages for users. Moreover, the slow loading speed of pictures has been solved using my previous studies on coding and programming. Apart from improving on professional coding aspects, I have asked for advice among club members to set up unique columns such as specialized event register entrance and communication section to increase more connection among members for a better relationship during the pandemic and long term development of the club.Therefore, our club has been developed rapidly on campus with immediate updated information and a friendly online environment.

  • The most significant challenge I have faced was taking AP Physics 1 & AP Physics 2 in junior years. The previous test results were in a mess, and it was hard to understand the lesson with a lack of fundamental physics background well established in former semesters. I was depressed and stuck in the situation to overcome it as our physics teacher reminded us to set up an appropriate goal and keep working for it. He compared the studies of physics problems to growing trees, and it was crucial to aim at the root for watering, which is like figuring out individual capacities and repeatedly working for it. He said that when things are easy, you redo it; when things become challenging, you practice when it is easy, which strongly inspired me. With the inspiration of the physics teacher, I started to brace myself up. First, I collected previous test papers we did the last term to do it again daily. Furthermore, I invited one of my friends to accompany me. We made a study pair to encourage each other. Every afternoon after the class, we open a zoom meeting to do homework, explain problems to each other. We collected mistakes we made in a file to review every day as a routine. Multiple choice and free response parts were two main difficulties to overcome in the final test. With slight improvement but still unsatisfied results, we constantly worked harder in the winter break. I spent three hours studying Physics, redid the old tests, and searched for some multiple-choice questions online to practice my weakness. Even before the AP test and second semester final, my friend and I spent extra five hours a day working on specific physics problems. As a result, the result for the second semester raised a grade with all AP tests passed. Thus, to look back on my studies, I become firmly assured that I could be one with the accurate academic aim and constantly work for it to achieve my long-term educational and career goal.
